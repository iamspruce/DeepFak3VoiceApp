Want to use your AI-generated voice during live video calls, streaming, or gaming? You'll need to route the audio through a "virtual microphone".

Works with: Zoom, Discord, Skype, OBS, and most calling/streaming apps!
Time needed: 5-10 minutes one-time setup
Distros: Ubuntu, Fedora, Arch, and most distributions

Step 1: Install PulseAudio Volume Control

Most Linux systems have PulseAudio. We'll use a control panel to manage it.

For Ubuntu/Debian:

sudo apt update
sudo apt install pavucontrol

For Fedora:

sudo dnf install pavucontrol

For Arch:

sudo pacman -S pavucontrol

Press Enter and type your password when asked.

Step 2: Create a Virtual Audio Device

Open Terminal and run these commands one at a time:

pactl load-module module-null-sink sink_name=VirtualMic sink_properties=device.description=Virtual_Mic

Then:

pactl load-module module-loopback source=VirtualMic.monitor
‚úÖ This creates a "Virtual Mic" that apps can use!

Step 3: Make It Permanent (Optional but Recommended)

So it works every time you restart your computer:

  1. Open Terminal
  2. Type: nano ~/.config/pulse/default.pa
  3. Press Enter (this opens a text editor)
  4. Scroll to the bottom (use arrow keys)
  5. Add these two lines: 
    load-module module-null-sink sink_name=VirtualMic sink_properties=device.description=Virtual_Mic
load-module module-loopback source=VirtualMic.monitor
  6. Press Ctrl + X
  7. Press Y (to save)
  8. Press Enter

Step 4: Set Your System Audio

Using GUI:

  1. Open Sound Settings (search in your system settings)
  2. Go to Output devices
  3. Select "Virtual_Mic" or "Null Output"

Using pavucontrol:

  1. Type in Terminal: pavucontrol
  2. Go to the "Output Devices" tab
  3. Set "Virtual_Mic" as the fallback device

To hear audio yourself AND send to calls:

In Terminal, run:

pactl load-module module-combine-sink sink_name=Combined

Then set "Combined" as your output in Sound Settings.

Step 5: Configure Your Calling App

For Zoom:

  1. Open Zoom ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Audio
  2. For Microphone, select: "Monitor of Virtual_Mic"

For Discord:

  1. Open Discord ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Voice & Video
  2. For Input Device, select: "Monitor of Virtual_Mic" or "Virtual_Mic Monitor"

For OBS:

  1. In OBS, go to Settings ‚Üí Audio
  2. For Mic/Auxiliary Audio, choose: "Monitor of Virtual_Mic"

For other apps:

Look for audio settings and choose "Monitor of Virtual_Mic" as the microphone/input

Step 6: Test It!

  1. Open DeepFak3r Vibe Voice
  2. Generate and play a voice
  3. Check if your calling app picks it up!
üí° Switching back to your real microphone:

Go to your app's audio settings and change the input device back to your normal microphone. You can switch back and forth anytime!
