Want to use your AI-generated voice during live video calls, streaming, or gaming? You'll need to route the audio through a "virtual microphone".

Works with: Zoom, WhatsApp, Discord, Skype, Teams, OBS, Streamlabs, and most calling/streaming apps!
Time needed: 5-10 minutes one-time setup

Step 1: Download VB-CABLE (Virtual Audio Driver)

  1. Go to: vb-audio.com/Cable
  2. Click "Download" under "VB-CABLE Virtual Audio Device"
  3. Extract the downloaded zip file
  4. Right-click VBCABLE_Setup_x64.exe (or VBCABLE_Setup.exe)
  5. Choose "Run as administrator"
  6. Click "Install Driver"
  7. Click "OK" when it says "Installation Complete"
  8. Restart your computer

Step 2: Download Voicemeeter (Audio Mixer)

This lets you hear audio yourself while sending it to calls.

  1. Go to: vb-audio.com/Voicemeeter
  2. Download "Voicemeeter" (the basic free version, not Banana or Potato)
  3. Extract the zip and run the installer as administrator
  4. Click "Install"
  5. Restart your computer again

Step 3: Set Up Voicemeeter

  1. Search "Voicemeeter" in the Start menu and open it
  2. The Voicemeeter window has lots of buttons ‚Äì don't worry, we only need a few!

Configure outputs:

  • In the top right corner, find "A1"
  • Click the dropdown menu under A1
  • Select your speakers or headphones (like "Speakers", "Realtek", or your headphone name)
  • Next to A1, find "B1"
  • Click the dropdown under B1
  • Select "CABLE Input (VB-Audio Virtual Cable)"

That's it for Voicemeeter setup! You can minimize it (keep it running).

Step 4: Set Windows to Use Voicemeeter

  1. Right-click the speaker icon in your taskbar (bottom right corner)
  2. Choose "Open Sound settings" or "Sounds"
  3. Under "Output" (or "Playback"), select: "Voicemeeter Input (VB-Audio Virtual...)"
  4. Under "Input" (or "Recording"), you can leave your regular microphone
‚úÖ Now all your computer's audio goes through Voicemeeter!

Step 5: Configure Your Calling/Streaming App

For Zoom:

  1. Open Zoom
  2. Click your profile picture ‚Üí Settings
  3. Go to Audio
  4. For Microphone, select: "CABLE Output (VB-Audio Virtual Cable)"
  5. For Speaker, leave your normal speakers
  6. Uncheck "Automatically adjust microphone"

For WhatsApp Desktop:

  1. Open WhatsApp
  2. Go to Settings
  3. For Microphone, choose: "CABLE Output (VB-Audio Virtual Cable)"

For Discord:

  1. Open Discord
  2. Click the gear icon (User Settings)
  3. Go to Voice & Video
  4. For Input Device, select: "CABLE Output (VB-Audio Virtual Cable)"
  5. Turn OFF "Automatically determine input sensitivity"

For OBS/Streamlabs:

  1. In OBS, go to Settings ‚Üí Audio
  2. For Mic/Auxiliary Audio, choose: "CABLE Output (VB-Audio Virtual Cable)"

Step 6: Test It!

  1. Keep Voicemeeter open (minimized is fine)
  2. Open DeepFak3r Vibe Voice
  3. Generate and play a voice
  4. You should hear it through your speakers
  5. The audio also goes to your calling app!

Volume too low in calls?

  • Open Voicemeeter
  • On the left side, find the sliders labeled "HARDWARE INPUT"
  • Move them up to increase volume sent to calls
