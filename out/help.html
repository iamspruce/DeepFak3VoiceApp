Part 3: Using Generated Voices in Calls (Zoom, WhatsApp, Discord, etc.)

Want to use your AI-generated voice during live video calls, streaming, or gaming? You'll need to route the audio through a "virtual microphone" so other apps can hear it.

What this does: Makes your computer treat DeepFak3r's audio output as a microphone input.

Time needed: 5-10 minutes one-time setup

Works with: Zoom, WhatsApp, Discord, Skype, Teams, OBS, Streamlabs, and most calling/streaming apps!

For Mac Users

Step 1: Download BlackHole (Free Virtual Audio Tool)

  1. Go to: existential.audio/blackhole
  2. Download BlackHole 2ch (the smallest version, easiest to use)
  3. Open the downloaded .pkg file
  4. Follow the installation steps (click Continue â†’ Install)
  5. Restart your Mac when the installation finishes

Step 2: Create a Multi-Output Device (So You Hear Audio Too)

If you only set BlackHole as output, you won't hear anything yourself! This step lets you hear AND send to calls.

  1. Press Command + Space and type: "Audio MIDI Setup"
  2. Open the Audio MIDI Setup app
  3. Click the "+" button at the bottom left corner
  4. Choose "Create Multi-Output Device"
  5. In the right panel, you'll see a list of audio devices
  6. Check the boxes for:
    • âœ… BlackHole 2ch (this sends audio to other apps)
    • âœ… Your speakers (like "Built-in Output", "MacBook Speakers", or your headphones)
  7. Make sure BlackHole 2ch is checked first (important!)
  8. Close the window (settings save automatically)

Step 3: Set Your Mac to Use This Setup

  1. Click the Apple menu (top left corner)
  2. Go to System Settings (or System Preferences on older macOS)
  3. Click Sound
  4. Go to the Output tab
  5. Select "Multi-Output Device" from the list

Now your Mac will play audio through speakers AND send it to virtual mic!

Step 4: Configure Your Calling/Streaming App

For Zoom:

  1. Open Zoom
  2. Click your profile picture â†’ Settings
  3. Go to Audio
  4. For Microphone, select: "BlackHole 2ch"
  5. For Speakers, leave your normal speakers selected
  6. Uncheck "Automatically adjust microphone volume" (important!)

For WhatsApp Desktop:

  1. Open WhatsApp
  2. Click Settings (gear icon)
  3. Go to Audio & Video or Settings
  4. For Microphone, choose: "BlackHole 2ch"

For Discord:

  1. Open Discord
  2. Click the gear icon (User Settings) at bottom left
  3. Go to Voice & Video
  4. For Input Device, select: "BlackHole 2ch"
  5. Turn OFF "Automatically determine input sensitivity"

For OBS/Streamlabs (Streaming):

  1. In OBS, go to Settings â†’ Audio
  2. For Mic/Auxiliary Audio, choose: "BlackHole 2ch"

Step 5: Test It!

  1. Keep your calling app open
  2. Open DeepFak3r Vibe Voice
  3. Generate and play a voice clip
  4. You should hear it through your speakers
  5. Start a test call or check your app's audio test
  6. Your friend (or the test) should hear the generated voice!

Switching back to your real microphone:

  • Just go to your app's audio settings
  • Change Microphone back to "Built-in Microphone" or your mic's name

For Linux Users (Ubuntu, Fedora, etc.)

Step 1: Install PulseAudio Volume Control

Most Linux systems have PulseAudio. We'll use a control panel to manage it.

For Ubuntu/Debian:

sudo apt update
sudo apt install pavucontrol

For Fedora:

sudo dnf install pavucontrol

For Arch:

sudo pacman -S pavucontrol

Press Enter and type your password when asked.

Step 2: Create a Virtual Audio Device

Open Terminal and run these commands one at a time:

pactl load-module module-null-sink sink_name=VirtualMic sink_properties=device.description=Virtual_Mic

Then:

pactl load-module module-loopback source=VirtualMic.monitor

This creates a "Virtual Mic" that apps can use!

Step 3: Make It Permanent (Optional but Recommended)

So it works every time you restart your computer:

  1. Open Terminal
  2. Type: nano ~/.config/pulse/default.pa
  3. Press Enter (this opens a text editor)
  4. Scroll to the bottom (use arrow keys)
  5. Add these two lines: 
    load-module module-null-sink sink_name=VirtualMic sink_properties=device.description=Virtual_Mic
load-module module-loopback source=VirtualMic.monitor
  6. Press Ctrl + X
  7. Press Y (to save)
  8. Press Enter

Step 4: Set Your System Audio

Using GUI:

  1. Open Sound Settings (search in your system settings)
  2. Go to Output devices
  3. Select "Virtual_Mic" or "Null Output"

Using pavucontrol:

  1. Type in Terminal: pavucontrol
  2. Go to the "Output Devices" tab
  3. Set "Virtual_Mic" as the fallback device

To hear audio yourself AND send to calls:

In Terminal, run:

pactl load-module module-combine-sink sink_name=Combined

Then set "Combined" as your output in Sound Settings.

Step 5: Configure Your Calling App

For Zoom:

  1. Open Zoom â†’ Settings â†’ Audio
  2. For Microphone, select: "Monitor of Virtual_Mic"

For Discord:

  1. Open Discord â†’ Settings â†’ Voice & Video
  2. For Input Device, select: "Monitor of Virtual_Mic" or "Virtual_Mic Monitor"

For other apps:

  • Look for audio settings
  • Choose "Monitor of Virtual_Mic" as the microphone/input

Step 6: Test It!

Generate audio in DeepFak3r Vibe Voice and check if your calling app picks it up!

Troubleshooting Common Issues

Installation Problems

"Windows protected your PC" warning (Windows)

  • Click "More info" then "Run anyway"
  • Make sure you installed the certificate first (see Part 1, Step 1)

"Cannot open because it is from an unidentified developer" (Mac)

  • Follow Method 2 in the Mac section: System Settings â†’ Privacy & Security â†’ Open Anyway
  • Or try right-clicking the app and choosing "Open"

"Permission denied" (Linux)

  • Make sure you ran: chmod +x DeepFak3rVoice
  • Try running from Terminal: ./DeepFak3rVoice

License Key Issues

"Invalid license key"

  • Double-check you copied the entire key from your Gumroad email (no extra spaces)
  • Make sure you're connected to the internet
  • Contact support@deepfak3r.com with your purchase email

"License already activated"

  • Each license works on 3 devices. If you've used all 3, contact support to reset
  • Make sure you're not running multiple copies of the app at once

App Performance Issues

App is very slow or crashes

  • Close other programs to free up RAM
  • Use RunPod cloud computing instead (see Part 2) â€” this solves most performance issues!
  • Make sure you have at least 2GB free disk space
  • Restart your computer and try again

"Out of memory" error

  • Your computer doesn't have enough RAM
  • Use RunPod (see Part 2) â€” highly recommended for this issue!
  • Close browser tabs and other apps before generating voices

Generated voice sounds robotic or distorted

  • Try using a higher quality audio sample (at least 10 seconds, clear speech)
  • Check your internet connection if using RunPod
  • Adjust quality settings in the app (if available)

RunPod Issues

"Could not connect to RunPod"

  • Check your API key is copied correctly (no spaces at the beginning/end)
  • Make sure you have credit in your RunPod account
  • Check your internet connection
  • Try clicking "Disconnect" then "Connect" again

"Deployment failed"

  • RunPod might be experiencing high demand. Wait 5 minutes and try again
  • Check RunPod's website to see if there are any outages
  • Your account might need more credit

Still using credit after closing the app

  • Open RunPod dashboard â†’ Pods
  • Manually terminate any running pods
  • The app should auto-stop them, but sometimes it doesn't if it crashes

Virtual Audio / Calling Issues

No sound in Zoom/WhatsApp/Discord

  • Double-check you selected the correct virtual microphone in the app's audio settings
  • Restart the calling app after changing settings
  • Make sure DeepFak3r Vibe Voice is actually playing audio
  • Check the volume isn't muted in the app

I can't hear the audio myself

  • Mac: Make sure you created the Multi-Output Device and selected it (Part 3, Steps 2-3)
  • Windows: Make sure Voicemeeter is running and A1 is set to your speakers
  • Linux: Use the Combined sink setup (see Part 3, Step 4)

People hear an echo in calls

  • Use headphones instead of speakers
  • Or lower your speaker volume
  • Turn off any "echo cancellation" features in your calling app

Want to use your real voice again

  • Just go to your calling app's audio settings
  • Switch the microphone back to "Built-in Microphone" or your mic's name
  • You can switch back and forth anytime!
